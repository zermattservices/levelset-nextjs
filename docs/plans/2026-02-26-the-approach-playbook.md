## 2. Audience Profile & Pain Points

### 2.1 Who You're Talking To

**Operators (franchise owners):**
- Own and operate 1–3+ CFA locations
- Business owners making purchasing/technology decisions
- Focused on ROI, operational efficiency, and business growth
- Think in terms of sales, customer satisfaction scores, and team stability
- Average revenue per location: ~$8.5M/year
- They're the check-signers

**Talent Directors:**
- Hired by Operators to own the talent strategy
- Day-to-day responsibility for hiring, development, retention, culture
- Many are SHRM-certified or pursuing certification
- They're the ones who will USE Levelset daily
- They feel the pain of spreadsheets, binders, and inconsistent processes most acutely
- They're your champion — they'll go back and sell it to their Operator if they love it

### 2.2 The Pain Points This Conference Is Addressing

Every session at The Approach surfaces problems that Levelset solves. Here are the core pains:

| Pain Point | How It Shows Up | How Levelset Solves It |
|------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Inconsistent team evaluation** | Different managers rate people differently. No standard for "what good looks like." | PEA ratings use position-specific criteria with a clear 1-3 scale. Every rater uses the same rubric. |
| **Retention beyond 90 days** | Team members leave after the honeymoon phase. No system to track engagement. | Ratings track performance trends over time. Leaders can see who's growing vs. plateauing and intervene early. |
| **No development pipeline** | Can't identify who's ready for shift leader, trainer, or director roles. | Rolling averages and position mastery data show exactly who's crushing it and ready for more. |
| **Discipline inconsistency** | One manager gives a warning, another fires for the same thing. | CARES progressive discipline system ensures consistent, documented accountability with recommended actions at each threshold. |
| **Spreadsheet chaos** | Ratings live in binders, discipline in folders, rosters in spreadsheets. Nothing connects. | One unified platform: roster, ratings, discipline, all linked per employee. |
| **Multi-location visibility** | Operators with 2-3 locations can't compare performance across restaurants. | Multi-location dashboard with cross-restaurant reporting. |
| **Employer branding** | Can't articulate or prove that they're a great place to work. | Data-driven performance culture that team members can see and feel. Position mastery gives clear growth paths. |
| **Generational expectations** | Gen Z wants clear expectations, frequent feedback, and visible growth paths. | PEA delivers exactly this: clear criteria per position, regular ratings, and visible progress. |
| **Training effectiveness** | No way to measure if training programs are actually working. | Before/after rating data shows if training translates to performance improvement. |
| **Manager accountability** | No visibility into whether supervisors are actually developing their teams. | Rater attribution shows who's rating, how often, and whether their teams are improving. |

### 2.3 Conversation Starters by Persona

**For Operators (think ROI and business impact):**
- "How are you tracking whether your team development investments are actually paying off?"
- "If I asked you right now who your top 3 team members are at each position, how quickly could you answer that?"
- "How do you ensure your managers are applying discipline consistently across shifts?"

**For Talent Directors (think daily workflow and credibility):**
- "What does your current process look like for tracking position-based performance?"
- "How much time do you spend each week compiling team performance data?"
- "When an Operator asks you 'how's the team doing?' — how do you answer that with data?"

## 3. Marketing Strategy & Positioning

### 3.1 Core Positioning

**The one-liner:** "Levelset is the team performance platform built exclusively for Chick-fil-A — turning your ratings, discipline, and roster into one connected system."

**The elevator pitch (30 seconds):**
> "You know how every restaurant has their own way of tracking ratings — some use binders, some use spreadsheets, some just do it in their heads? Levelset connects all of that into one platform. Your positions, your criteria, your discipline process, your entire roster — all in one place. Every rater uses the same standards, every team member sees clear expectations, and you get real-time data on how your team is actually performing. We built it specifically for Chick-fil-A because no other tool understands your operational model."

**The 10-second hook (for when they're walking by):**
> "We're Levelset — we replace the binders and spreadsheets CFA operators use to track team performance. Want to see it?"

### 3.2 Messaging Pillars

**1. Built for CFA, not adapted to CFA**
- Not a generic HR tool with CFA branding. Built from the ground up for CFA positions, criteria, and workflows.
- You understand FOH/BOH, the PEA framework, CARES discipline, and shift leadership development.

**2. Consistency across your entire team**
- Every supervisor rates using the same criteria. Every discipline decision follows the same process.
- No more "it depends on who's managing that day."

**3. Data that drives decisions**
- Replace gut feelings with rolling averages, position mastery scores, and trend data.
- Answer "how's the team doing?" with numbers, not narratives.

**4. Your team members can feel the difference**
- Clear expectations per position. Visible growth paths. Fair, documented accountability.
- This is what Gen Z is asking for — and what retains them beyond 90 days.

### 3.3 Competitive Differentiation

You're not competing against other software vendors at this event. You're competing against **the status quo**: binders, spreadsheets, and "how we've always done it." Your positioning should acknowledge this:

- "We're not trying to add another tool to your stack. We're trying to replace the 3-4 disconnected things you're already doing."
- "If your system is working great and your data is clean and accessible, you don't need us. But if you're spending hours compiling reports or worrying about consistency — that's what we solve."

### 3.4 Social Proof (Use What You Have)

You have two real operators using Levelset. Use them:
- "We're already live with CFA operators who helped us build this. The platform was literally shaped by operators like you — it's not theoretical."
- If Riley Emter or Reece Howard are willing to be referenced by name, that's gold. A single text or quote from them saying "this changed how we manage our team" is worth more than any marketing material.

**Action item:** Before the event, ask both operators if they're comfortable being named as references. Even better, ask if they'd be willing to take a 30-second video testimonial on their phone.

### 3.5 Tie-ins to Conference Sessions

This is your secret weapon. When someone walks into the Talent Hub after a session, you can reference what they just learned:

- After **Nido Qubein's employer branding keynote**: "He talked about making your restaurant a place people choose. Levelset gives you the data to prove you're investing in your people — clear criteria, tracked growth, fair accountability."
- After **Tim Elmore's generational session**: "He mentioned Gen Z wants frequent feedback and clear expectations. That's literally what PEA ratings deliver — position-specific criteria rated regularly."
- After **Chris McChesney's execution session**: "He taught the Four Disciplines of Execution. Levelset is your scoreboard — it makes team performance visible and trackable so your strategy actually gets executed."
- After **SHRM onboarding labs**: "They covered 30/60/90 onboarding. Levelset tracks that progression with ratings data — you can see if your onboarding is actually producing capable team members."
- After **retention/engagement labs**: "That session was about keeping people beyond day 90. Levelset shows you exactly who's plateauing so you can intervene before they disengage."

## 4. Demo Strategy

### 4.1 Demo Flow (3-Minute Version)

You have about 3 minutes of someone's attention at a vendor booth. Here's the optimal flow:

**[0:00–0:30] Hook — The Problem**
"So how are you tracking team performance right now — spreadsheets, binders, or just in your head?" (Let them answer. They'll laugh or groan.)

**[0:30–1:30] Show — The Dashboard**
Open the Levelset dashboard:
1. Show the **employee roster** — "Here's your whole team in one place."
2. Click into an employee — "Every rating, every position, their history. No digging through binders."
3. Show **PEA ratings** — "Your supervisors rate on your exact positions using your exact criteria. Register, drive-thru, grill — whatever your restaurant runs."
4. Show the **rating scale** — "Simple 1-3: Not Yet, On the Rise, Crushing It. Rolling averages from the last 4 ratings so you see trends, not just snapshots."

**[1:30–2:15] Wow — The Insight**
5. Show the **position performance view** — "Across your whole team, here's how you're doing at each position. See where you're strong, see where you need development."
6. Show **discipline tracking** — "Same person, all their infractions, documented consistently. The system recommends the next action based on points — no guessing."

**[2:15–3:00] Close — The Bridge**
"Everything I just showed you was built by CFA operators specifically for how CFA restaurants run. We're in early access right now — I have a special offer for Approach attendees to get set up with a free trial. Want me to grab your info so I can send you the link?"

### 4.2 Demo Flow (1-Minute Quick Version)

For when someone's only half-stopping:

"We're Levelset — we built a platform that replaces the binders and spreadsheets CFA operators use for team ratings and discipline. Let me show you one thing." → Show the position-based rating screen → "Every position, clear criteria, rolling averages. Your whole team in one view." → "Here's a QR code — scan it and I'll send you a free trial link."

### 4.3 Demo Data Requirements

Your demo org needs to look real and impressive. It should include:

- **15–25 employees** with realistic names across FOH and BOH roles
- **Multiple positions** filled out: Register, Drive-Thru, Dining Room, Grill, Prep, Fries, Beverages, Breading
- **Rating history** for each employee spanning at least 8–12 weeks (to show rolling averages and trends)
- **Mix of performance levels**: some "Crushing It" across the board, some "On the Rise" improving, some "Not Yet" needing development
- **2–3 discipline records** showing the progressive system (verbal coaching → written warning)
- **Multiple raters** to show rater attribution
- **At least 2 locations** to demo multi-location view if applicable

**The demo org should tell a story.** When someone looks at it, they should see patterns that feel familiar — "oh, we have someone like that" — and immediately understand the value.

### 4.4 Demo Environment Setup

- **Laptop 1 (your primary):** Full dashboard logged in to the demo org. This is your main demo machine.
- **Laptop 2 (hands-on):** Same demo org, oriented toward the visitor. Let them scroll through it themselves. People sell themselves when they can touch the product.
- **iPad (optional, if brought):** Could show the mobile rating submission flow — "this is what your supervisors use on the floor to submit a rating in under 30 seconds."

**Critical:** Make sure the demo org URL is bookmarked and the session won't time out. Test WiFi at the venue early on Sunday during setup. Have a mobile hotspot as backup if hotel WiFi is unreliable.

| Question/Objection | Response |

| "I need to talk to my Operator / Talent Director." | "Totally understand. Let me grab your info and send you a link you can share with them. The demo is self-explanatory — they'll get it in 2 minutes." |
| "Does it integrate with [HotSchedules / Paycom / etc.]?" | "We're building integrations — scheduling sync with HotSchedules is on our roadmap. Right now the platform handles ratings, discipline, and roster as a standalone system. Most operators tell us that alone saves them hours per week." |

## 5. Lead Capture & Conversion Funnel

### 5.1 The Funnel

```
ATTRACT → DEMO → CAPTURE → BRIDGE → CONVERT
(booth)   (3min)  (info)   (promo)   (post-event)
```

You can't close full signups on-site because onboarding requires their own computer, logins, and files. So the goal is: **make the demo so compelling that they go set up their trial that night in their hotel room or the week they get home.**

### 5.2 Lead Capture Method

**Primary: QR Code → Simple Form**
Create a simple landing page or form (Google Form, Typeform, or a page on levelset.io) with:
- Name
- Email
- Restaurant name / location
- Role (Operator or Talent Director)
- "What interested you most?" (optional — helps personalize follow-up)

Display the QR code prominently at your booth. When someone's done with the demo, say: "Scan this and I'll send you the trial link plus a recap of everything I showed you."

**Secondary: Phone + Notes**
For deeper conversations, just ask "Can I grab your number? I'll text you the link." Take a quick note on your phone about what they were most interested in. Personal texts from a founder convert better than marketing emails.

**Backup: Business Cards / Paper**
Have a small notepad as backup for anyone who doesn't want to scan or give their number. Write down their name and email.

### 5.3 The Approach Offer

Create a special offer exclusive to Approach attendees. This serves two purposes: (1) incentivizes action, and (2) creates urgency.

**Recommended offer: "The Approach Launch Package"**
- Free 30-day trial (full access, no credit card required)
- Personalized onboarding call with you (the founder — this is a premium touch)
- Founding member pricing locked in if they subscribe during the trial
- "You'll be in our first cohort of operators — your feedback directly shapes the product"

**Why this works:**
- Free trial removes risk
- Personal onboarding from the founder makes it feel exclusive, not mass-market
- "Founding member" positioning turns early adoption into a badge of honor
- Feedback loop makes them feel like partners, not customers

### 5.4 The "Hotel Room" Conversion Path

Many attendees will have downtime in the evenings (especially Monday after 7pm when activities are optional). Your follow-up should be timed to hit during these windows:

- **Sunday evening (after 8pm):** Text anyone you spoke with during the Sunday Talent Hub window: "Great meeting you at The Approach! Here's the link to start your free trial: [link]. Takes about 15 minutes to set up. Happy to jump on a call anytime this week to walk you through it."
- **Monday evening (after 7pm):** Same for Monday contacts. The evening activities are optional — many will be in their rooms.
- **Tuesday afternoon (after 1:30pm):** Final batch. "It was great connecting at The Approach. Here's your trial link before you head home: [link]"

### 7.5 Energy Management (Solo Operator)

You're alone for 3 days. Burnout is real. Here's how to manage:

- **Hydrate and eat.** Meals are included — don't skip them to staff the booth. Your energy matters more than catching one extra conversation.
- **Use forum sessions as breaks.** When everyone's in the ballroom, you have 60–90 minute rest windows. Use them to sit, eat, review notes, and recharge.
- **Stand, don't sit**, when the Hub is open. Standing is more approachable and projects energy.
- **Don't force it.** If someone's not interested, let them go with a smile and a handout. Save your energy for engaged prospects.
- **Evening networking is optional.** If you're drained Monday night, skip glow golf and rest. You need energy for Tuesday.

### 9.1 Same-Day Follow-Up (Critical)

The #1 mistake vendors make at conferences is waiting too long to follow up. By the time you send an email 5 days later, they've forgotten you. Follow up the SAME DAY you talk to someone.
