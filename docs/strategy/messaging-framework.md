# Levelset Messaging Framework

> Last updated: 2026-03-01
> Audience: Internal strategy document for marketing, sales, and product alignment.

## Target Market

**100% Chick-fil-A restaurants. No exceptions.**

- ~2,900 US locations (TAM)
- Franchise model: each location has one Operator (owner)
- Multi-unit operators manage 2-5+ locations
- Average 80-120 employees per location, high turnover industry
- Heavy reliance on paper, pencil, spreadsheets, and disconnected tools

## The Core Problem

Chick-fil-A Operators are running complex team management across disconnected systems:

- **Ratings** on paper or spreadsheets — no rolling averages, no trends, no accountability
- **Discipline** tracked in binders — inconsistent application, no escalation tracking
- **Scheduling** across separate tools — disconnected from performance data
- **Development** conversations happen verbally — no documentation, no follow-through
- **Pay** decisions are ad-hoc — no transparent, performance-based system
- **AI** is not applied to restaurant management — leaders spend hours on tasks Levi handles in seconds

**The result:** Leaders spend more time managing systems than developing people. Team members don't see a clear path forward. Retention suffers.

## CFA-Specific Language Guide

### ALWAYS Use These Terms
| Levelset Term | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| **Operator** | CFA franchise owner (never "owner" or "manager") |
| **Team Member** | Entry-level employee (never "worker" or "staff") |
| **Team Lead** | First leadership role (never "shift lead" or "supervisor") |
| **Trainer** | Training-qualified team member |
| **Director** | Senior leadership (never "assistant manager") |
| **Executive** | Top leadership below Operator |
| **FOH / BOH** | Front of House / Back of House (standard CFA zones) |
| **iPOS** | Primary order-taking position |
| **Drive-Thru** | Not "drive through" |
| **Positional Excellence** | The rating system (never just "ratings" in marketing copy) |
| **OE Pillars** | Operational Excellence pillars (5 CFA-aligned dimensions) |
| **The Big 5** | The 5 rating criteria per position |

### NEVER Use These Terms in Marketing
| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **PEA** | Reece Howard org-specific term, NOT CFA standard | Positional Excellence, ratings |
| **CARES** (for discipline) | CARES is CFA's customer complaint department, NOT a discipline term. Misusing it shows we don't understand CFA. | Progressive Discipline, discipline system |
| Employee management | Too corporate/generic | Team management, team development |
| HR software | Positions us wrong | Team management platform |
| Staff | Not CFA terminology | Team members |
| Workers | Too impersonal | Team members |
| Manager | CFA uses specific role titles | Operator, Director, Team Lead |
| Performance review | Generic HR term | Evaluation, Positional Excellence |
| KPIs | Corporate jargon | OE Pillars, ratings, position averages |
| Workforce management | Enterprise/generic | Team management |

### CFA Terms to Know (but NOT necessarily use in marketing)
| Term | What It Actually Means |
|------|----------------------|
| **CARES** | CFA's customer complaint/resolution department. Customers "send a CARES" when they complain to corporate. CARES handles customer issues, escalates food safety / upset customer cases to the restaurant. **This is NOT a discipline term.** |
| **PEA** | "Positional Excellence Assessment" — a term used by specific operators (Reece Howard) for their rating system before Levelset. NOT a CFA-wide term. Use "Positional Excellence" or "ratings" instead. |

> **ACTION NEEDED:** The Approach Playbook (`docs/plans/2026-02-26-the-approach-playbook.md`) contains multiple references to "PEA" and "CARES" in ways that don't align with CFA terminology. These should be corrected before the event to avoid credibility issues with Operators.

## Audience Segmentation

### Primary: Operators (Decision Makers / Check-Signers)
- **Pain:** Time spent managing disconnected systems, inconsistent processes across locations
- **Motivation:** Develop team members, drive operational excellence, retain talent, protect their investment
- **Message:** "Stop managing systems. Start developing people. Everything connected in one platform."
- **CTA:** See a demo, start a free 30-day Pro trial
- **Revenue context:** Average CFA location does ~$8.5M/year. $249/month is trivial.
- **Key question to ask:** "If I asked you right now who your top 3 team members are at each position, how quickly could you answer that?"

### Secondary: Talent Directors (Daily Users / Champions)
- **Pain:** Manual rating tracking, inconsistent discipline, no development documentation, binders and spreadsheets
- **Motivation:** Better tools to develop their teams, credibility with data, less paperwork
- **Message:** "Your team's performance, development, and coaching — all in one place, always up to date."
- **Key insight:** Talent Directors feel the pain most acutely. They're the ones who will USE Levelset daily. They sell it to their Operator.
- **Key question to ask:** "When your Operator asks 'how's the team doing?' — how do you answer that with data?"

### Tertiary: Multi-Unit Operators (Enterprise / Highest ACV)
- **Pain:** Inconsistency across locations, no visibility into team metrics across restaurants
- **Motivation:** Standardize processes, compare locations, scale what works
- **Message:** "One platform across all your locations. Same standards. Same visibility. Connected."
- **Revenue context:** A 3-location Operator on Pro = $747/month ($8,964/year). 550 multi-unit Operators control 40% of all locations.
